What? Why? How?

What ?


CO2 Emission Predictor is a web application that predicts the carbon emission from vehicles.
It is a web based application that uses linear regression algorithm for the prediction of carbon dioxide emissions.
A simple to use portal that asks user for their vehicle information and returns the amount of possible carbon dioxide emission.
It also dynamically visualizes the datasets used for the very prediction.
Last but not least, it stores the user inputs for further references.

Currently, the carbon emission information is not very transparent in the context of our country. As it stands, our city happens to be the 6th most polluted city in the world in terms of air pollution index. Co2 Emission Predictor focuses on this very matter by providing a platform for all public to view the carbon emission from pre-existing data set or predict the carbon emission from their own vehicle. This will not only raise awareness, but diminish the stigma of petroleum vehicle preferences. It is an efficient web application that makes use of a highly studied dataset for the calculation of carbon emission using linear regression algorithm. The main purpose of this project is to give the carbon emission value in the most accurate way as possible and save our time in doing extensive research for each and every vehicle. Further, this project aims to showcase a visualization of the pollution emitted by the vehicles, be it car or motorbike.

Why ?


A typical passenger vehicle emits about 4.6 metric tons of carbon dioxide per year. Globally, 80% of CO2 emission is from combustion of fossil fuels mainly in manufacturing and transportation industry. Our city currently ranks 6th in the most polluted city of the world. There are many problems caused in our day-to-day life due to this alarming situation. And the very main reason for this problem, is unawareness by the general public about their ecological footprint. In many cases, people just go about their day-to-day life without a care about the carbon emitted by their vehicle. The objectives of this project are to:

1. Help user predict carbon emission from their vehicle.
2. To provide a simple portal that takes multiple information (at least 3) about their vehicle for prediction.
3. To visualize the carbon emission from the existing dataset.
4. To promote EVs, or any other vehicle that produce less emission.


How ?


Linear regression is a quiet and simple statistical regression method used for predictive analysis and shows the relationship between the continuous variables. Linear regression shows the linear relationship between the independent variable (X-axis) and the dependent variable (Y-axis), consequently called linear regression. If there is a single input variable (x), such linear regression is called simple linear regression. And if there is more than one input variable, such linear regression is called multiple linear regression. The linear regression model gives a sloped straight line describing the relationship within the variables.




The above graph presents the linear relationship between the dependent variable and independent variables. When the value of x (independent variable) increases, the value of y (dependent variable) is likewise increasing. The red line is referred to as the best fit straight line. Based on the given data points, we try to plot a line that models the points the best.
To calculate best-fit line linear regression uses a traditional slope-intercept form.

Things you could do.


Choose EVs


By choosing to drive an EV you are helping to reduce harmful air pollution from exhaust emissions. An EV has zero exhaust emissions, but still creates a degree of greenhouse gas emissions when it is charged from the electricity grid.


Choose BS6


To regulate the pollution emitted by cars and two-wheelers, the government has put forth regulations known as Bharat Stage Emission Standards. A BSVI engine outputs 60% less emission as compared to BS4 engine and is considered a much better alternative.


Choose Public Transit


Taking public transit not only reduces the carbon emission but also promotes a healthier traffic in the community. Furthermore, promoting EVs and public transit could very well end the emission problem altogether.
